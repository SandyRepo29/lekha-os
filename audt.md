# AUDT Strategic Positioning & Product Blueprint

# 1. Category Definition

## Category

**Vendor Governance & Trust Operations (VGTO)**

## Definition

A Vendor Governance & Trust Operations platform enables organizations to discover, assess, govern, monitor, audit, and continuously validate the trustworthiness of third-party vendors, suppliers, SaaS applications, service providers, and AI providers.

The platform acts as the system of record for every vendor relationship and provides a unified operating model for:

* Vendor Governance
* Vendor Risk Management
* Compliance Management
* Audit Management
* Trust Operations
* Continuous Monitoring

---

# Why This Category?

Most existing platforms start from one narrow problem:

| Vendor            | Starts With      |
| ----------------- | ---------------- |
| Vanta             | Compliance       |

| Sprinto           | Compliance       |
| UpGuard           | Vendor Risk      |

AUDT starts with the vendor itself.

Everything else becomes a workflow around that vendor.

# 2. Vendor Governance & Trust Operations Lifecycle

```text
Discover
↓
Inventory
↓
Classify
↓
Assess
↓
Risk
↓
Comply
↓
Monitor
↓
Audit
↓
Remediate
↓
Renew
↓
Offboard
```

This lifecycle becomes the foundation of the entire platform.

Every module should fit into one or more stages.

---

# Stage 1 — Discover

Goal:

Identify all vendors used across the organization.

Questions:

* Who are our vendors?
* What SaaS tools do we use?
* Which vendors process company data?

Outputs:

* Vendor Inventory
* Vendor Registry

---

# Stage 2 — Inventory

Goal:

Create a complete system of record.

Questions:

* Who owns the vendor?
* What contract exists?
* What data do they access?
* What is the annual spend?

Outputs:

* Vendor Master Record
* Vendor Profile

---

# Stage 3 — Classify

Goal:

Determine vendor criticality.

Questions:

* Does the vendor process PII?
* Does the vendor access production systems?
* Is the vendor business critical?

Outputs:

* Vendor Tier
* Vendor Criticality
* Vendor Category

---

# Stage 4 — Assess

Goal:

Evaluate vendor security and compliance posture.

Questions:

* SOC2?
* ISO27001?
* Security controls?
* Privacy controls?

Outputs:

* Assessments
* Questionnaires
* Evidence Collection

---

# Stage 5 — Risk

Goal:

Calculate vendor risk.

Questions:

* What is the inherent risk?
* What is the residual risk?
* What controls reduce risk?

Outputs:

* Risk Score
* Risk Register
* Risk Treatment Plan

---

# Stage 6 — Comply

Goal:

Demonstrate compliance.

Questions:

* Which controls apply?
* Which evidence exists?
* Which frameworks are covered?

Outputs:

* Compliance Status
* Framework Coverage
* Audit Readiness

---

# Stage 7 — Monitor

Goal:

Continuously monitor vendor trustworthiness.

Questions:

* Has the vendor been breached?
* Has compliance expired?
* Has risk increased?

Outputs:

* Monitoring Alerts
* Vendor Health Score
* Continuous Assurance

---

# Stage 8 — Audit

Goal:

Provide assurance.

Questions:

* Was vendor assessed?
* Is evidence available?
* Were findings remediated?

Outputs:

* Audit Package
* Auditor Workspace
* Audit Reports

---

# Stage 9 — Remediate

Goal:

Resolve issues and findings.

Outputs:

* CAPAs
* Issues
* Exceptions
* Action Plans

---

# Stage 10 — Renew

Goal:

Make informed renewal decisions.

Questions:

* Should we renew?
* Should we replace?
* Should we renegotiate?

Outputs:

* Renewal Recommendation
* Vendor Performance Review

---

# Stage 11 — Offboard

Goal:

Safely terminate vendor relationships.

Questions:

* Was access revoked?
* Was data deleted?
* Was the contract terminated?

Outputs:

* Offboarding Checklist
* Data Destruction Validation

---
